How AQI is Calculated in India

The Air Quality Index (AQI) in India is calculated based on eight pollutants:

PM2.5 (Fine Particles)Particles ≤ 2.5 micrometers
PM10 (Coarse Particles)Particles ≤ 10 micrometers
NO₂ (Nitrogen Dioxide)Traffic & industrial emissions
SO₂ (Sulfur Dioxide)Combustion of fossil fuels
CO (Carbon Monoxide)Incomplete combustion
O₃ (Ozone)Ground-level ozone
NH₃ (Ammonia)Agricultural activities
Pb (Lead)Industrial sources

AQI Categories:

0–50: Good

Minimal impact

51–100: Satisfactory

Minor breathing discomfort to sensitive people

101–200: Moderate

Breathing discomfort for lung/heart disease patients

201–300: Poor

Breathing discomfort on prolonged exposure

301–400: Very Poor

Respiratory illness on prolonged exposure

401–500: Severe

Seriously impacts people with existing diseases

The final AQI is determined by the pollutant with the highest sub-index value.
